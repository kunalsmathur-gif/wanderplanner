# The full work

## 1. Market snapshot

WanderPlanner sits inside India's travel-planning layer — the step before booking. The country's overall tourism economy is large and growing (**$231.6B / ₹21T contribution to GDP in 2024**, 8th globally — [Invest India, verified](https://www.investindia.gov.in/sector/tourism-hospitality)), but almost none of that is planning-tool revenue; it's flights, hotels, and packages. Three structural facts shape everything below:

- **Planning itself is a Tax Job** — a necessary chore, not the thing travelers value or pay for directly. That's why direct-to-consumer subscription is structurally the weakest of the segments below.
- **Travel funding is rotating toward B2B.** B2B travel-tech funding overtook B2C for the first time in 2024 (51%/49% split, per the earlier session's research) — the market itself is telling you where the money is.
- **A well-funded direct competitor already exists** ([Mindtrip](https://mindtrip.ai), $22.5M raised, ~1.5M MAU, 30+ DMO partnerships) running exactly the "consumer free tool + B2B licensing" playbook this report recommends — proof the model works, and proof you're not first.

- **Cost/time:** ~2 weeks, mostly outreach and scheduling.

**Assumption 2: Agents will trust and act on an AI draft without redoing intake**
- **Method:** A concierge test — manually deliver AI-generated drafts to 2–3 agencies' real incoming leads for two weeks and observe actual behavior, not stated intent.
- **Steps:** Partner with 2–3 willing agencies, intercept a subset of their real inbound leads, generate a WanderPlanner draft, hand it to their agent, and track whether they used it largely as-is, edited lightly, or redid it from scratch.
- **Kill criterion:** if agents redo more than half the draft from scratch in the majority of cases, the value claim (saves iterations) is false as designed.
- **Cost/time:** 2–3 weeks in parallel with Assumption 1.

**Assumption 3: Unit economics close**
- **Method:** Back-of-envelope cost model using data you already have.
- **Steps:** Pull average Gemini cost per itinerary generation from the existing admin analytics dashboard; add estimated WhatsApp Business API cost per conversation; compare against the price point validated in Assumption 1.
- **Kill criterion:** if cost-to-serve exceeds 30–40% of the validated price at any realistic volume, the pricing or the delivery mechanism needs to change before building further.
- **Cost/time:** 1–2 days — this is desk research, not fieldwork.

**Assumption 4: The addressable agency count is real**
- **Method:** Desk research against named directories.
- **Steps:** Cross-check counts against [TAAI](https://taai.travel) membership listings and any published GST-registered travel-services counts.
- **Kill criterion:** if the digitally-reachable, willing-to-adopt count comes in under ~5,000, revisit the size claim in Section 2 before sizing revenue off the larger headline number.
- **Cost/time:** 2–3 days.

## Appendix — market sizing, and how to re-check it yourself

### A. The calculations

**TAM (India leisure-trip spend a planning tool could sit in front of) = ~$100–150B/yr**
- **Formula:** (outbound travelers × avg outbound spend) + (planned multi-day domestic trips × avg domestic trip spend)
- **Inputs:**
  | Input | Value | Where it came from | Assumption / confidence |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Indian outbound travelers/yr | ~27–30M, rising toward ~40M by 2027 | Industry-cited estimate (not independently verified this pass) | Analog/guess — cross-check against [UNWTO](https://www.unwto.org/) and Ministry of Tourism data |
  | Avg outbound trip spend | ~$1,500–2,000 | Prior session research (industry-cited) | Analog — verify against travel-industry reports |
  | Domestic tourism visits/yr | ~2B+ | Widely-cited Ministry of Tourism range | Unverified this pass |
  | Share that are "planned multi-day leisure trips" (vs. short/pilgrimage visits) | ~10–15% | Pure assumption | Guess — needs a real source |
  | Avg domestic planned-trip spend | ~$180–300 (₹15,000–25,000) | Assumption | Guess |

**SAM (digitally-native travelers who'd use an AI chat planner) = ~6–10M/yr**
- **Formula:** ~20–30% digital-first share of the outbound + domestic-planned pool above
- No hard source — a placeholder until validated by actual traffic/conversion data post-launch.

**SOM (B2B agency revenue, Year 3, realistic) = ~$120K–900K ARR**
- **Formula:** (agencies in segment) × (adoption rate) × (annual price)
- **Inputs:**
  | Input | Value | Where it came from | Assumption / confidence |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Small agencies in India | 50,000–100,000+ | Rough estimate | Unverified — wide range, see Assumption 4 above |
  | Realistic Year-3 adoption | 200–500 agencies (0.4–1% of the low end) | Assumption, no benchmark yet | Guess |
  | Annual price per agency | $600–1,800/yr ($50–150/mo) | Analog to small-business SaaS pricing in adjacent categories | Assumption — untested, see Assumption 1 |

### B. Re-check it yourself

**Re-check the agency count and SOM:**
1. **Count the agencies.** Check [TAAI](https://taai.travel) chapter membership counts and any published GST-registered "travel agency" services classification counts. → compare to our 50,000–100,000 range.
2. **Get the price.** Look at adjacent small-business SaaS tools sold to Indian SMEs (invoicing tools, CRM-lite tools) for a realistic monthly price band an owner-operator would accept. → compare to our $50–150/mo assumption.
3. **Bound the adoption rate.** Use early-stage B2B SaaS benchmarks (0.1–1% of a cold TAM converting in year 1–3 without a strong existing channel) — [OpenView](https://openviewpartners.com/) and [First Round Review](https://review.firstround.com/) publish general SaaS benchmarks usable as a rough analog. → compare to our 0.4–1% assumption.
4. **Redo the multiply.** Agencies × adoption × price. Within ~30% of our number confirms the order of magnitude; off by more than 2× means challenge the agency-count input first — it's the weakest link in this chain.

**Job budget (what one agency spends per year on this today):** the fastest real check is inside the interviews in Assumption 1 above — ask directly what an owner currently spends in staff time and tools on drafting itineraries, rather than estimating it further from outside.
